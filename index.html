Speakers

Alicia Carr

We're excited to welcome self taught developer Alicia Carr from Atlanta, GA. Some years back, in a chance moment whist purchasing an iPad, the grandmother of 8 met a teenager who learned how to build an app watching YouTube videos. Enthralled by the idea, she enrolled shortly after in an Objective C course. This eventually led her to build an app focused on a topic near and dear to her heart: a domestic violence app. Purple Evolution, Inc (PEVO) App was established as an effort to empower women experiencing domestic violence with the essential tools required to develop a safe and secure exit plan. Her personal breakthrough and STEM success story has led her to be an invited guest at Apple's WWDC, as well as being included in campaigns by both Apple and GitHub. A staunch supporter and advocate of women in tech, she has spoken at conferences from Austin to Australia, Italy to Ireland, and from Monterrey Mexico to what will be her 1st visit ever to Toronto, where she plans to narrate her personal campaign and goal of empowering women.

Huda Idrees

Huda is the Founder and CEO of Dot Health, a real-time personal health data platform. With a background in engineering from the University of Toronto and a prolific career at some of Toronto's best known startups, she hopes to transform the way people access and control their own health information for good. A strong advocate for women and minorities in technology, Huda's work been profiled in numerous media outlets including The Globe and Mail, Metro News, CNBC, and Metro Morning. Huda's work in the field of entrepreneurship recently earned her a spot as a Top 5 CEO in Toronto Life Magazine. She was also recognized as one of Top 25 Women of Influence in 2017. Huda serves on the Business Innovation and Development committee at Sinai Health System, and the Canadian Federal Economic Strategy Table for Health and Biosciences.
